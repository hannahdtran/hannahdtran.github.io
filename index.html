Hi, my name is Hannah.

I'm currently a third-year student at the University of Texas at Dallas. I'm majoring in Biology with a minor in Secondary Science Education.

Currently, I'm studying for my second DAT attempt in March and preparing to enter the spring semester of junior year. When I'm not studying or working, I like to volunteer, scrapbook, sing karaoke or try new foods!

I'm a huge fan of creating things (a major part of why I'm interested in dentistry!), so I made this website to chronicle my undergraduate years. Join me on my pre-dental journey!

A bit about me

I was born and raised in the suburbs of Dallas, Texas, and I have a sister who is 2.5 years younger than me. We're like two peas in a pod. My favorite color is pink, and my favorite movie is Ratatouille because of its inspiring theme: "Anyone can cook!" While I'm not entirely sure what my favorite food is, I think it might be mangos. My family immigrated to the United States about 30 years ago due to the Vietnam War. I've always admired their perseverance and have drawn inspiration from it in everything I do. This has helped me appreciate the beauty in hardship, which is one of the main reasons I'm interested in dentistry.

Growing up, science was my favorite subject because of how concrete and applicable it is, and this naturally led me toward healthcare. I gained a deeper understanding of the field in high school when I participated in a clinical rotations program. At first, I was drawn to medicine, captivated by the fast-paced environment of hospitals. However, I soon realized I wanted a better work-life balance, so I pivoted to pharmacy, then to optometry. During my first year at university, I became heavily involved in the UTDallas pre-optometry club and attended statewide optometric conferences to learn more about the field. However, everything changed when an unexpected job opportunity in dentistry came my way, leading me to finally switch my focus to dentistry.

My Why

While there are many aspects of dentistry that I appreciate, one overarching theme connects most of my reasons for pursuing dentistry: the ability to connect and comfort patients in the most direct way. Working and volunteering as an RDA for the past 2.5 years has allowed me to immerse myself into the intricacies of dentistry. I deeply value the continuity of care that dentistry facilitates. I’ve had the privilege of following my own patients’ journeys at work—celebrating their newborn grandkids, college admissions, post-graduation paths and even inspiring some to consider dentistry!

I admire how a collection of radiographs tells a story…and how dentists get to add to that story year after year. Dentists have the ability facilitate an environment of comfort and trust for every patient that enters the office simply due to the nature of the profession: it focuses on prevention and patient education, prioritizing patient choice.

To sum everything up, my experiences have reinforced my belief in the power of dentistry to affect change, no matter how small. From allowing patients to stray from their treatment plan to providing free dental work to hundreds of patients at mass volunteer events, dentistry makes way for profound impacts and compassionate care, which drives my dedication to the field.
